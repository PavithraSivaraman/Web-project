Bird Sanctuaries of India

1. Bharatpur Bird Sanctuary (Keoladeo National Park)

Located in Rajasthan, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its diverse bird species, especially migratory birds.

2. Chilika Lake Bird Sanctuary

Situated in Odisha, it is the largest coastal lagoon in India and a hotspot for migratory birds.

3. Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary

Located in Kerala, it is famous for its backwaters and a variety of migratory birds, especially during the winter season.

4. Manas Bird Sanctuary

Part of the Manas National Park in Assam, it is known for its rich biodiversity and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

5. Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary

Located in Karnataka, it is known for its large population of migratory birds and is a popular eco-tourism destination.

6. Vedanthangal Bird Sanctuary

Situated in Tamil Nadu, it is one of the oldest bird sanctuaries in India, attracting a variety of migratory birds.

7. Nal Sarovar Bird Sanctuary

Located in Gujarat, it is known for its large wetland area and diverse bird species, especially during the winter months.

8. Bhindawas Bird Sanctuary

Situated in Haryana, it is a man-made wetland that attracts a variety of migratory and resident birds.

9. Salim Ali Bird Sanctuary

Located in Goa, it is named after the famous ornithologist Salim Ali and is known for its rich avian diversity.

10. Kutch Desert Wildlife Sanctuary

Located in Gujarat, it is known for its unique desert ecosystem and a variety of bird species, including flamingos.
