Caves of India

1. Ajanta Caves

Located in Maharashtra, these rock-cut caves are famous for their ancient Buddhist paintings and sculptures, dating back to the 2nd century BCE.

2. Ellora Caves

Also in Maharashtra, Ellora features a mix of Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain rock-cut temples and is known for the impressive Kailasa temple.

3. Badami Caves

Located in Karnataka, these caves are known for their rock-cut temples and intricate carvings, primarily dedicated to Hindu deities.

4. Karla Caves

Situated in Maharashtra, these ancient Buddhist rock-cut caves are known for their chaitya (prayer hall) and intricate sculptures.

5. Bhimbetka Caves

Located in Madhya Pradesh, these caves are famous for their prehistoric rock paintings, showcasing the life of early humans.

6. Elephanta Caves

Situated on Elephanta Island near Mumbai, these caves are known for their rock-cut sculptures dedicated to the Hindu god Shiva.

7. Udayagiri and Khandagiri Caves

Located in Odisha, these caves are significant for their historical and archaeological importance, with many Jain inscriptions.

8. Leni Caves

Situated in Maharashtra, these rock-cut caves are known for their ancient Buddhist sculptures and inscriptions.

9. Pandav Leni Caves

Located in Maharashtra, these caves are known for their rock-cut architecture and are believed to have been inhabited by Buddhist monks.

10. Naneghat Caves

Situated in Maharashtra, these caves were used as a trading route and are known for their inscriptions and rock-cut sculptures.
