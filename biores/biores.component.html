Biosphere Reserves of India

1. Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve

Located in Tamil Nadu, Kerala, and Karnataka, it is known for its rich biodiversity and is home to several endangered species.

2. Nanda Devi Biosphere Reserve

Situated in Uttarakhand, it encompasses the Nanda Devi National Park and is known for its unique flora and fauna.

3. Manas Biosphere Reserve

Located in Assam, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its diverse wildlife, including the Indian rhinoceros.

4. Sundarbans Biosphere Reserve

Famous for its mangrove forests and Royal Bengal tigers, it is located in West Bengal and is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

5. Gulf of Mannar Biosphere Reserve

Located in Tamil Nadu, it is known for its rich marine biodiversity and coral reefs.

6. Great Nicobar Biosphere Reserve

Situated in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, it is known for its unique ecosystems and endemic species.

7. Pachmarhi Biosphere Reserve

Located in Madhya Pradesh, it is known for its rich biodiversity and scenic beauty.

8. Khangchendzonga Biosphere Reserve

Located in Sikkim, it is known for its stunning landscapes and diverse flora and fauna.

9. Achanakmar-Amarkantak Biosphere Reserve

Situated in Madhya Pradesh and Chhattisgarh, it is known for its rich biodiversity and is part of the Amarkantak plateau.

10. Similipal Biosphere Reserve

Located in Odisha, it is known for its rich wildlife, including tigers and elephants, and diverse plant species.
