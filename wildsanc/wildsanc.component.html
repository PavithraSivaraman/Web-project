Wildlife Sanctuaries of India

1. Jim Corbett Wildlife Sanctuary

Located in Uttarakhand, it is the oldest national park in India and is famous for its Bengal tigers.

2. Kaziranga Wildlife Sanctuary

Situated in Assam, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its population of the Indian one-horned rhinoceros.

3. Bandhavgarh Wildlife Sanctuary

Located in Madhya Pradesh, it has one of the highest densities of tigers in the world.

4. Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary

Situated in Kerala, it is known for its elephants and scenic lake, attracting many tourists.

5. Nagarhole Wildlife Sanctuary

Located in Karnataka, it is known for its rich biodiversity and is part of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve.

6. Sundarbans Wildlife Sanctuary

Famous for its mangrove forests and Royal Bengal tigers, it is located in West Bengal and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

7. Ranthambore Wildlife Sanctuary

Located in Rajasthan, it is known for its tiger population and historical ruins within the park.

8. Keoladeo National Park (Bharatpur Bird Sanctuary)

Located in Rajasthan, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its diverse bird species and rich wildlife.

9. Simlipal Wildlife Sanctuary

Situated in Odisha, it is known for its rich wildlife, including tigers, elephants, and various bird species.

10. Achanakmar Wildlife Sanctuary

Located in Chhattisgarh, it is known for its diverse flora and fauna and is part of the Achanakmar-Amarkantak Biosphere Reserve.
